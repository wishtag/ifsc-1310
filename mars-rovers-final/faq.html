Frequently Asked Questions

How many rovers has NASA sent to Mars?

NASA has sent five successful rovers to Mars: Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.

What is the purpose of the Mars rover missions?

Each mission aims to explore the Martian surface, study its geology and climate, search for signs of past life, and prepare for future human exploration.

How do the rovers communicate with Earth?

The rovers communicate with Earth via satellites orbiting Mars, which relay data back to NASA's Deep Space Network.

Why was Jezero Crater chosen for Perseverance?

Jezero Crater is believed to have once contained a lake and river delta, making it a promising site for finding signs of ancient microbial life.

Can the rovers be controlled in real time?

No, due to the time delay in sending signals between Earth and Mars, rovers operate semi-autonomously with instructions uploaded by mission teams.
