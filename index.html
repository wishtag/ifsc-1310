Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement was a blueprint of a new way to approach web development that was more device inclusive than the “graceful degradation” approach it aimed to replace. Both of these approaches consider how well a website works on different platforms, but they look at this process differently. Graceful degradation focuses on having the best possible performance on the most current and powerful browsers and platforms. There is some support for older and less powerful browsers and systems, but it's more of an afterthought and doesn’t really see much focus or development time. Progressive enhancement looks at things differently. This approach focuses on content rather than strictly being concerned with how the site performs on specific browsers. Using the progressive enhancement mindset allows you to focus on your content and work your way out from there. The content of the site or application forms a base that allows you to build on in order to add features and platform-specific elements and support different browsers and platforms, but the main content remains the focus of your site or application. This approach is important today because the content is what people are looking for, and they don’t want to be handicapped by needing to be on a specific platform or device. We now live in a world where everyone is consuming content on any number of devices that could be mobile phones, tablets or computers from many different manufacturers that are running one of many different operating systems and browsers. In order for a company to survive, the content of their site needs to be accessible on all platforms and devices without issues.
